Physics Principles Behind the Air Purifier

Each stage of the purifier relies on well-established physics concepts to remove different types of pollutants. Explore how the science adds up to cleaner, safer air.

I. Mechanical Filtration

The pre-filter and HEPA filter work like fine nets, catching dust and tiny particles as air passes through.

II. Brownian Motion

Tiny particles move randomly and collide with the HEPA fibers, causing them to stick and get trapped in the filter.

III. Adsorption

Activated carbon has countless pores that attract VOC molecules and hold them in place, removing gases and smells.

IV. UV-C Light

It emits short-wavelength (~254nm) UV light, which damages the DNA and RNA of germs, stopping bacteria, viruses, and mold from growing and spreading.”

V. Airflow Dynamics

Smooth ducts and steady fans move air evenly so each filter can work well.
